Equipment Timeline
Before Phuket — Jul 2026
Mask — Gull Luvos (Shing) SGD 335 | Gull Coco (GF) already bought SGD 160
Shing: Gull Luvos SGD 315 + SGD 20 anti-fog lens = SGD 335. GF: Gull Coco (Black/Marble Shell Blue) SGD 140 already purchased + SGD 20 anti-fog lens = SGD 160. Fit is personal — can't rely on rental for every dive from here. Both masks are low-volume, ideal for freediving compatibility later.
Before Singapore Diving — Sep 2026
Wetsuit 3mm — Scubapro Definition SGD 390 each
Required for Singapore diving (water feels cold after 2+ hours). Saves SGD 20/pax/day rental. Why Scubapro Definition over others: uses Everflex neoprene (stretches more than standard neoprene, much easier to put on), better fit retention after repeated use, superior seam construction, noticeably more comfortable on multi-dive days. Cressi Fast (SGD 150–180) is the budget alternative but thinner neoprene quality shows after 20+ uses. If budget is tight, Cressi Fast is acceptable — but Definition is the better long-term buy.
Phuket Day 2+ — Jul 2026
Fins — Mares Avanti Quattro Plus (Shing, white) SGD 170 | Gull Super MEW-X Shell Blue (GF) price TBC
Buy at Phuket dive store after Day 1 — confirm comfort from Day 1 experience first. Shing: Mares Avanti Quattro Plus — stiff blade, GUE-compatible, excellent frog kick control. White, SGD 170. GF: Gull Super MEW-X in Shell Blue to match her Coco mask. Gull is a Japanese brand — may be cheaper and better stocked in Okinawa. Consider waiting to buy GF fins in Okinawa instead. Avoid Scubapro Seawing Supernova — hinged blade makes frog kicks harder, not GUE-compatible.
Before Tioman — Aug 2026 (or rent at SGD 10/dive)
Dive Torch — X-Adventurer M1800 SGD 120 each
Torch rental is SGD 10/dive — at this rate, buying breaks even at ~12 night dives which won't happen for a while. Renting is fine until you're doing frequent night dives or a liveaboard. However: having your own torch means you can confidently do night dives anywhere without worrying about availability. Buy before Tioman if you want reliability, or keep renting until Okinawa. Each diver needs one — backup torch also recommended before first night dive.
Before Tulamben — Sep/Oct 2026
Macro Lens — DIVEVERYDAY M67 adapter + M67 macro wet lens (~SGD 150–200 total)
Buy BEFORE Tulamben — not after. Tulamben's Seraya and Batu Niti sites have world-class macro: frogfish, ghost pipefish, harlequin shrimp, wonderpus octopus, nudibranchs. Without a macro lens the DJI Action 5 Pro cannot resolve small critters well in blue-green water — you'll shoot blurry nothing. With M67 macro wet lens, you get usable shots even at 40+ dives. At ~50 dives and 65+ buoyancy is stable enough to hover and shoot.
Okinawa — Nov 2026 (GF only, consider)
Fins — Gull Super MEW-X Shell Blue (GF)
Gull is Japanese brand — better selection, likely cheaper in Japan vs Singapore. If not bought in Phuket, Okinawa is the ideal place to buy GF's Gull fins.
~60–80 dives — Post Okinawa (Optional)
Regulator — Optional, ~SGD 500+ each
Hygiene and familiarity are the main reasons to own a reg. Rental regs are shared by many divers. At 60+ dives on bigger trips (liveaboards, Sipadan), own reg makes sense. Not urgent — rent everywhere cheaply until then. BCD likely defer indefinitely given travel style (heavy to fly with, cheap to rent in SE Asia at Rp 50,000/day).
~60–80 dives — After Singapore Phase 2 (assess)
Olympus TG-7 Underwater Camera (~SGD 600)
Dedicated underwater compact — better than action cam for macro stills, built-in microscope mode, RAW shooting. Makes sense when you've outgrown the macro wet lens on the Action 5 Pro. Assess after Singapore Phase 2: if getting good shots with the M67 macro setup, TG-7 may not be needed yet. If macro photography becomes serious (Lembeh trip planning), then yes. Not a priority until 60+ dives.
⚠️ What NOT to buy yet
  • BCD — heavy to travel with, SGD 5/day rental in Tulamben. Defer indefinitely given travel style.
  • Booties — Dive Concept rents for Rp 30,000 (SGD 3). Only needed with open-heel fins.
  • Scubapro Seawing Supernova fins — hinged blade fights against GUE frog kick technique.
  • Divevolk SeaTouch 4 — phone housing, separate system from DJI Action 5 Pro. Not needed while you have the action cam.
Certifications

  • Open Water — foundational cert, 18m depth limit
  • Advanced Open Water — 30m depth limit, unlocks most recreational sites

Deep Diver
HIGH PRIORITY
Where: Phuket — Aussie Divers
When: Jul 2026
Min. dives: ~25
Unlocks 40m. Essential before Boga Wreck in Tulamben (30m), USS Emmons in Okinawa (22–40m), and any serious wall/wreck diving. Do it properly — Aussie Divers actually teaches gas planning, nitrogen narcosis management, emergency procedures. Not a checkbox cert. This is the most important next cert.
Drift Diver
MEDIUM
Where: Nusa Penida or Sipadan
When: 2027
Min. dives: 50+
Unlocks confident current diving at Nusa Penida (strong unpredictable currents), Sipadan (drift along turtle walls), and Raja Ampat (Blue Magic current site). Not needed for Phuket or Tulamben. Natural next cert after Deep. Do alongside Nusa Penida trip in 2027 if skipped in 2026.
Wreck Diver
MEDIUM
Where: Tulamben or Coron
When: 2027
Min. dives: 50+
Unlocks wreck penetration diving — going inside wrecks, not just hovering outside. Makes Coron's 10 Japanese wrecks significantly more rewarding. USS Emmons penetration also benefits. Liberty wreck in Tulamben is an ideal training wreck. Do in Coron or on a return Tulamben trip.
GUE Basic Fundamentals
HIGH — Long term
Where: Singapore or KL
When: 2027–2028
Min. dives: 60–80
Cost: ~SGD 2,000 each
Resets your technique baseline — buoyancy, trim, propulsion, gas management done properly. Not a specialty cert, it's a philosophy. All current gear (Gull mask, Mares Avanti fins, Scubapro wetsuit) is already GUE-compatible. Entry point to tech diving if desired. Expensive but genuinely transforms your diving. Do only when you have own gear and 60–80 dives of muscle memory to reset.
❌ Not worth doing
  • Night Diver specialty — no unlock value, you'll do night dives naturally and the cert just formalises experience you already have. Save the money.
  • Peak Performance Buoyancy — useless on rental gear. Buoyancy is tuned to your specific setup. Wait until you own BCD + wetsuit consistently, then it sticks. By that point you'll have buoyancy from experience anyway.
